Color

The choice of Color should be related to some kind of relationship between the symbol and the concept it represents. Color is one of the most cognitively effective visual variables for the human visual system. Differences in color are detected three times faster than shape and are also more easily remembered. However it should not be used as the sole basis for distinguishing between symbols, as it is sensitive to variations in visual perception (e.g., color blindness), in screen/printer characteristics (e.g., B/W printer) and in representational mediums (e.g., whiteboards/paper).
